Smart Market Design.

I work at the intersection of computer science, mathematics, AI and economics, with a focus on solving practical problems using simulations and stochastic optimization.

I'm a 3rd year PhD student from AdONE (Advanced Optimization in a Networked Economy) , a training community targeting at the intersection of mathematics, computer science, and management science, at the Technical University of Munich, in Germany. I got my master degree from the University of Tokyo, Japan and spent years as a research assistant in National Institute of Informatics, Japan. Therefore, I have some background in theoretical computer science and applied economics. I have a deep interest in utilizing mathematical modeling and computing techniques (and AI) to design mechanisms, policy/data analysis, and development to markets (though not limited to).

My research is currently supported by Prof. Dr. Stefan Minner Prof. Dr. Stefan Minner (TUM School of Management, TUM, currently Editor-in-Chief of IJPE ) and Prof. Dr. Martin Bichler(Department of Informatics, TUM.). Meanwhile I am cooperating with Prof. Dr. Naonori Kakimura, Stefan Kober and Martin Bullinger extensively. I enjoy talking to people in academia or industry to find out how to better optimize this amazing world. I am very interested in market design that can be applied in practice.

Outside of research, I also have my own media channels (Bilibili, Weibo ) at some largest video-sharing websites in China. Sometimes I do vlogs to record my life, but I'm more interested in spreading more knowledge about operations research in practice.

Note to prospective PhD students: The CS department at the University of Maryland admits students to the department itself, not to an individual advisor. However, I will do my best to respond to inquiry emails.

Consider applying to the Maryland Max Planck PhD Program: spend time at both MPI in Germany and UMD in the US, work with tons of great folks, and get paid more!
